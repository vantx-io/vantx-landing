## Design Context

### Users
CTOs and engineering managers at scaling companies (Series A-C, 50-500 employees) who lack dedicated performance engineering, SRE, or observability talent. They're overwhelmed by alert noise, blind to production performance, and can't justify a $150K+ full-time hire. They arrive at vantx.io after searching for fractional SRE or performance engineering services, often frustrated by months of failed recruiting or underwhelming consultancies.

### Brand Personality
**Precise, Confident, Calm.** The senior engineer who has seen this problem a hundred times and knows exactly what to do. Not loud, not salesy — just competent. Speaks in specifics (tools, deliverables, timelines), not abstractions. Every word earns its place.

### Emotional Goal
**Relief.** "Finally, someone who gets it." The CTO should feel the tension in their shoulders release. The copy should name their exact pain, then immediately demonstrate that Vantx has the specific expertise to solve it. Not urgency-driven ("act now!") — trust-driven ("here's exactly what we do, here's what it costs, here's how fast we start").

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Light, warm, premium. Parchment backgrounds, forest green accent, generous whitespace. Left-aligned asymmetric layouts. Restrained color — one accent, used with intention.
- **References**: DesignJoy / Brett.dev (productized service clarity, warm personality, subscription-forward pricing) blended with Datadog / PagerDuty (technical credibility, domain expertise, no-nonsense).
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS templates (gradient heroes, stock photos, "trusted by 10,000+" badges, identical card grids). Dev tool landing pages (dark mode default, terminal aesthetics, neon accents, code snippets as decoration).
- **Theme**: Light mode primary, dark mode via `prefers-color-scheme` (warm darks, not cold/blue).
- **Typography**: DM Sans (body — warm geometric sans) + JetBrains Mono (numbers and data only). Monospace reserved strictly for pricing, stats, and code abbreviations — never for prose labels.

### Design Principles
1. **Name the pain, then solve it.** Don't talk about capabilities in the abstract. Show the specific problem ("alerts firing at 3 AM"), then the specific fix ("actionable alert triage, weekly").
2. **Precision over polish.** Exact deliverables, exact prices, exact timelines. A CTO trusts specifics more than visuals. No vague "transform your infrastructure" language.
3. **Quiet confidence.** No exclamation marks, no urgency tactics, no "limited spots." The design should feel like a senior engineer's portfolio — understated, competent, zero need to impress.
4. **One accent, used with intention.** Forest green appears on CTAs, key data, and the accent headline. Everything else is warm neutrals. Color scarcity creates impact.
5. **Subscription clarity.** Pricing is visible, not hidden behind "contact us." The model is DesignJoy-inspired: flat monthly rate, pause or cancel anytime, no hourly tracking.
